About Me

I am a junior studying Computer Science at Cornell University, advised by Prof. Claire Cardie and Wenting Zhao. My research lies at the intersection of natural language processing, reinforcement learning, and machine learning. I have also collaborated with Prof. Thorsten Joachims and PhD student Zhaolin Gao on LLM frameworks for academic review generation.

Research

My research focuses on developing language models that can reason effectively across multi-hop questions and fact verification tasks. I'm currently working on the Reasoning Court (RC) Framework to enhance LLM accuracy on complex reasoning benchmarks. I'm also exploring automated dataset curation for LLM self-improvement and culturally contextualized text generation systems.

Previously, I worked on algorithmic music composition and image transformation techniques, with publications on utilizing mathematical sequences for musical composition and developing lightweight GANs for image cartoonization.
