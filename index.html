About Me

I am a junior majoring in Computer Science and minoring in Artificial Intelligence at Cornell University, where I am fortunate to be advised by Professor Claire Cardie. I have also collaborated with Professor Thorsten Joachims and Ph.D. student Zhaolin Gao on developing a demonstration system for LLM-based academic review generation.


Research

My research interests lie in improving the reasoning capabilities, factual accuracy, and adaptability of large language models (LLMs). I am currently developing frameworks to enhance the multi-hop reasoning performance of LLMs and exploring techniques for automated high-quality dataset curation aimed at realizing self-evolving models. Additionally, I have been exploring LLM-driven automated code generation systems using Aider, and I am investigating the application of LLMs to generate lyrics that are contextualized both culturally and temporally.
